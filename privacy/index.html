1. Information stored on the device

BitGhost stores gameplay state, settings, and local diagnostics inside the app sandbox on the player's device. This includes save snapshots, theme preference, notification preferences, and local telemetry or debug data used to inspect run health on-device.

In the current build, this project does not run its own external analytics backend for gameplay telemetry. Local telemetry files are kept on-device unless the player or developer chooses to export them manually for debugging or support.

2. Purchases and advertising

The app uses Apple's in-app purchase system for the one-time Stable License purchase. Purchase availability, transaction status, and restore information are handled by Apple under Apple's own policies and terms.

The app also includes rewarded ad support through Google AdMob and Google's User Messaging Platform. If rewarded ads are enabled in a production build, Google may process device, advertising, consent, or ad interaction information as described in Google's own privacy materials.

3. Notifications

If a player opts in, BitGhost may schedule local notifications such as breached-node reminders or Ghost Cycle readiness reminders. Notification preferences are stored on-device and can be changed at any time in iOS Settings or in the app if that control is available.

4. Children's privacy

This site is not directed to children under 13. If you believe a child provided personal information directly to the developer, contact the support email below and the developer will review the request.

5. Third-party services

  • Apple App Store and StoreKit for app distribution and in-app purchases.
  • Google AdMob for rewarded advertising.
  • Google User Messaging Platform for ad consent and privacy options flows where applicable.
  • GitHub Pages to host this public website.

6. Contact

For privacy questions, data requests, or support issues, contact: replace-with-your-support-email@example.com

This policy is a practical launch version for a small indie game. Update it whenever the app starts sending data to a new third-party service or backend.
